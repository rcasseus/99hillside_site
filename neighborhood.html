The Location

Located at the northern tip of Manhattan, Inwood Terrace, is 25 minutes from Midtown and a few steps away from the Dyckman Street IRT #1 and a short walk from the IND A subway stations. Inwood Terrace is centrally located to colleges, universities and hospitals and is convenient to all shopping and houses of worship. The Cloisters (a branch of the Metropolitan Museum of Art), Fort Tryon Park, Wave Hill, and Inwood Hill Park are also easily accessible.

The People

The cooperators at Inwood Terrace represent a variety of cultures and backgrounds. Engineers, artists, teachers, secretaries, entrepreneurs, homemakers and electricians have happily chosen to call Inwood Terrace home. They have in common the opportunity to live in a safe, secure and friendly environment without giving up financial freedom.

About the Building

The Building

Erected in 1960, Inwood Terrace, Inc. a middle income Mitchell Lama cooperative, is a 21 story residential building containing 204 spacious 1, 2, and 3 bedroom apartments. Amenities include, on-site maintenance crew, indoor parking (wait listed), on- site laundry room, discount cable service, community room, storage room, bike room and private park/playground.

The Financial Advantage

The cost of a one-bedroom apartment in Manhattan ranges from $270,000 to $700,000, with monthly maintenance fees of $700-$2,000. The Mitchell Lama program makes the prospect of living in Manhattan realistic and attainable. At Inwood Terrace, Inc. the average one bedroom purchase price is $32,000 with a monthly maintenance fee of approximately $540 (including gas and electric utilities).
